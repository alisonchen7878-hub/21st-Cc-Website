About

Alison Chen is a violinist, violist, educator, and entrepreneur who believes in music’s power to build connection and spark change. Her career has taken her from the stage to classrooms and communities across the world, where she has used music as a tool for healing, learning, and empowerment.

As both a performer and teacher, Alison has worked with diverse communities, including leading music therapy workshops in Africa and teaching in underprivileged communities in Brazil through her company, Readysetgo Music. Her entrepreneurial path reflects the same creativity she brings to her artistry: designing programs that meet community needs, managing teams, and building projects that make a tangible difference.

On stage, Alison has been recognized with first prizes at the Princeton University Pitch Competition and Ivey Pitch Competition. She has performed across North America as a soloist, chamber musician, and orchestral player.

Currently completing a dual degree at Western University in Business and Music, Alison continues to bridge the two disciplines.
