About

Alison Chen is a violinist, violist, educator, and entrepreneur who believes in music’s power to build connection and spark change. Her career has taken her from the stage to classrooms and communities across the world, where she has used music as a tool for healing, learning, and empowerment.

As both a performer and teacher, Alison has worked with diverse communities, including leading music therapy workshops in Africa and teaching in underprivileged communities in Brazil through her company, Readysetgo Music. Her entrepreneurial path reflects the same creativity she brings to her artistry: designing programs that meet community needs, managing teams, and building projects that make a tangible difference. These experiences deepened her understanding of music’s role beyond performance halls—it can be a source of resilience, creativity, and belonging. She carries this vision into every project she undertakes.

On stage, Alison has been recognized with first prizes at the Princeton University Pitch Competition and Ivey Pitch Competition for her work as an entrepreneur. She has performed across North America as a soloist, chamber musician, and orchestral player, exploring repertoire that spans classical traditions and contemporary works. Her work as a conductor—self-taught through dedication and mentorship—has allowed her to lead ensembles and refine her ability to inspire collaboration through clear vision and communication.

Currently completing a dual degree at Western University in Business and Music, Alison continues to bridge the two disciplines, shaping a career that integrates performance, teaching, and innovation. She is passionate about developing projects that connect music to broader communities, especially in ways that expand access and bring people together.

Looking ahead, Alison hopes to continue performing while building arts initiatives that center on education, accessibility, and social impact. Whether on stage or in the community, her work is guided by the belief that music has the power to transform lives.

Teaching

Private violin and viola instruction for students of all ages, from beginners to advanced performers.

Studio Overview

Alison teaches RCM levels from beginner through ARCT, working with children, teens, and adults. Lessons are tailored to each student’s goals, whether that means exam preparation, orchestral auditions, chamber music, or personal growth.

Instruction is available both in-person in the Greater Toronto Area and online, making it possible for students to study from a variety of locations and schedules.

Approach

  • Strong technical foundations and healthy setup
  • Focus on sound, phrasing, and musical storytelling
  • Structured practice strategies and goal-setting
  • Preparation for RCM exams, auditions, and performances
  • Supportive, collaborative studio environment
